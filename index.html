Game Dev

I'll put stuff here.

Game Dev is something that the Tech Team has been working on for a long time, and have slowly getting more and more pieces together. It has been slowly developed since early 2012, and has been expanded into a lot more. It currently is in the process of planning and programming, and a lot of work has been done on the project in recent months.

Game Dev is such a large project because it encompases almost all of the interests that people have in the club. Programming for the code. Graphics and 3-D design for the characters. Audio for the sounds. It grabs a little piece of everything, which is exactly how it is in the real world when making a game. It is a learning experience as well, as most of the people working on the project has never worked on the piece that they are working on.

In order to make the game function, there needs to be a chosen programming language. Because different people in the club know different languages, we are making TWO version of the same game, in different languages, and then when it is done, we will decide as a team which is the best one to release for people to play.

A prototype should be available for download and play in the coming weeks. If you are interested in testing it, or just playing it, stay tuned for more information on what happens next!

  • Project Lead: Michael Ziluck
  • Category: Programming/Design

Tech Team Policy

All members and probationary members agree to this.

Preamble

The Newtown High School Technology Team, in order to preserve order and promote a more productive and effective Team, do establish this Policy for the current and future Newtown High School Technology Team.

The Technology [Tech] Team, from here on referred to as the ‘Team’, exists to provide a social medium for the exchange of knowledge among students with a preexisting understanding and a never-ending curiosity regarding technology of all kinds, as well as a learning environment to increase the Team’s ability as a whole.

Members from here on refer to people who are active in the club, and follow all expectations and requirements as defined in Article 2.

Probationary Members, also known as Probies, from here on refer to people who are active in the Team but have not been voted in as a Member.

Article I - Responsibilities

Section I - Member Responsibilities

Members of the Team will be expected to take responsibility for any of their own actions, including, but not limited to, directions given to Probationary Members, activity in productions, attendance to meetings, and other Team tasks they take part in. Activity in all aspects of the Technology Team must be evident: members are expected to attend both meetings a week, and required to attend at least one. If a reason is given to the Secretary prior to or within 2 days of the meeting, it will be recorded as an excused absence, and will not count toward grounds of a strike. Failure to contact the Secretary will result in the absence being marked as unexcused. After 5 unexcused absences, a strike will be brought up against the accused during the next meeting they attend. Members are required to either attend, or edit, at least one event or 5 hours worth a month depending on amount of available events. Members are also expected to be working on Personal Projects, progress on which is to be shown at select meetings.

Section II - Elected Positions

Elected Positions are President, Vice President, Treasurer, Equipment Manager, Secretary, Broadcast Director, Technical Director, and Project Manager. Elections will occur no more than two months prior to the end of the school year, and any position that is not filled in the Election will be appointed within the first month of the following school year. It is the responsibility of all Members to vote on those who will fill these roles yearly. It is also the responsibility of those not in an official position to work with those who are, and follow their decisions. Should a decision be questioned, a vote may be proposed, and majority ruling within the Team will enable it to be overturned.

Section III - Room and School Policies

Tech Team has claim over and uses the rooms B253 (Referred to as Lockdown) and B132 (Referred to as Headend). The cleanliness and presentability of these rooms falls under the responsibility of all Members and Probationary Members, however. All members of Tech are required to follow the rules that come with the room as designated by that years executives. The room, although not being overseen by an adult must be treated as though there is one in the room. This includes, but is not limited to keeping a regular voice level, lack of profanity, no exceptions for public displays of affection, or lewd or sexual conduct. Only one non-Tech Team individual per Member/Proby present may be in the room at any given time, and no more than five non Tech Team persons may be in the room at any time, regardless of Tech Team members in the room.

Article II - Membership

Section I - Membership Outline

Membership in the Tech Team is primarily earned through trust as well as contribution and display of ability. Candidates for membership are required to work with, and contribute to, the Team, in order to be eligible for membership. A candidate will be immediately expected to begin working at becoming a Member when the Probationary Member displays their continued desire (attending two meetings) to become a part of the Tech Team.

Membership is the official association of the Team with a Member. A Member will have access to B253, the resources of the Tech Team as designated by their access level, an ID card allowing them to exit out of study halls and cafeterias, and the ability to vote upon various Team decisions (including, but not limited to, the induction of new Members).

A failed attempt at membership does not immediately preclude Probationary Members from subsequent attempts, except in extenuating circumstances as defined by the current President and Vice President. In the event that they are not voted in, they are given a one month trial period, after which they are able to choose to be voted on again at their discretion.

Section II - Membership Requirements

It is the duty of all existing members of the Team to evaluate and observe Probationary Members in all school areas on their behavior, participation, work ethic, general participation, and any other qualities that members of the Team would require. Once a Probationary Member becomes a Member, these expectations are not dropped. In order to remain a member of the Team, they must follow the responsibilities mentioned in Article 1 Section 2 and adhere to all school rules outlined in the Student Handbook. This includes, but is not limited to, unexcused absences or tardiness in school, bullying of other students and/or staff, harassment in any form of other students and/or staff, theft, vandalism, and wearing inappropriate clothing.

Section III - Membership Expectations

All Members must adhere to standards of the Tech Team in the following: professional standards of behavior and participation of events (including but not limited to Board of Education meetings, Team meetings, school-sanctioned sports events, and staff related services), upholding personal responsibilities and Team-wide responsibilities, continuous activity within the Team, and anything defined by the sitting President or Vice President as an expectation.

Article III - Safety and Security

Section I - Strike System

Any Member/Probationary can bring up a strike against another Member, anonymously or otherwise. In the case of a rule violation by a Probationary Member, the prosecution may propose the punishment to the sitting President or Vice President. Strikes may be brought up based on violation of Team Policy, violation of School Rules, or if they believe it would be in the Team’s best interest. The Team may vote once both parties have made their case, however, the President may not vote; he or she resides as an impartial judge to moderate discussion and determine appropriate punishment in addition to the Strike. After a Member receives a strike they will have their card revoked for two weeks as a baseline. Additional punishment or scale decided on by the team, proposed by president. When they receive their second strike they receive the punishments of first strike plus a demotion to probationary status. Third strike permanently removed from the club for the remainder for the year.

Section II - Safety and Trust

All Members of the Team, including Probationary Members, represent the Team when operating at any school event or using the rooms. In addition to that, all full Members represent the Team at times other than the two aforementioned locations. Physical altercations between Team Members has a zero-tolerance policy, and successful verification of events will result in suspension of one or both parties from the Team for a period of no less than two weeks. The extent of the punishment will be determined by the sitting President or Vice President. Serious threats, especially those made in jest without obvious lack of intent, are extremely unprofessional and grounds for strikes. Haphazard equipment handling can lead to damage or loss of equipment and harm to people, thus is also grounds for a Member to be stricken.

Article IV - Policy Overview

Section I - Changes and Modification

This document may only be revised in the case that the supermajority vote to do so. Minor changes may be voted on by the current Elected Position holders, while major changes must be voted on by all present members of the Team. Major changes would include, but are not limited to, redefinition of positions, redefinition of how Membership is attained, redefinition of how a Position is attained, redaction or addition to the current placed system, etc. Minor changes would include, but are not limited to, spell corrections, formatting corrections, etc. All Members who have previously agreed to the Terms also agree to any future additions so long as they remain a Member of the Team.
